European Commission Buried Report Detailing AI-Driven Disinformation Campaigns Orchestrated by China

Leaked internal documents reveal the EU suppressed findings on Beijing's sophisticated AI-powered efforts to influence elections and sow discord, fearing diplomatic repercussions.

Editor P-A Gustafsson December 9, 2025 · 8 min read

The halls of the European Commission are once again echoing with accusations of political expediency overriding security concerns. An explosive report, meticulously detailing China's deployment of advanced Artificial Intelligence to generate and amplify disinformation campaigns targeting European elections and social stability, was allegedly shelved by the von der Leyen administration. Internal documents, leaked to *The Daily Signal* by a source within the Commission's Directorate-General for Communications Networks, Content and Technology (DG CONNECT), paint a picture of deliberate suppression aimed at avoiding a diplomatic row with Beijing.

The Suppressed Report: Project Nightingale

The report, codenamed "Project Nightingale," was the culmination of an 18-month investigation led by Dr. Anya Sharma, a specialist in AI-driven threat analysis within DG CONNECT's Security and Trust unit. Her team's findings, detailed in a draft summary seen by *The Daily Signal*, conclude that Chinese state-sponsored actors have been leveraging sophisticated AI models to create highly realistic deepfakes, generate targeted propaganda, and automate the spread of disinformation across social media platforms. These campaigns, according to the report, were specifically designed to exploit existing societal divisions within EU member states, undermine trust in democratic institutions, and influence electoral outcomes.

Project Nightingale identified several specific AI tools allegedly utilized by Chinese operatives. These include advanced generative adversarial networks (GANs) capable of creating realistic audio and video deepfakes of political figures, natural language processing (NLP) models used to generate hyper-personalized propaganda targeting individual voters, and sophisticated bot networks that amplify disinformation narratives and harass opposing viewpoints. The report also detailed evidence suggesting that these AI tools were being deployed in multiple languages, indicating a coordinated, pan-European disinformation campaign.

One particularly alarming finding was the identification of a previously unknown AI model, dubbed "Silk Road," that appears to be specifically designed to bypass existing content moderation systems on social media platforms. According to the report, Silk Road uses advanced adversarial techniques to subtly alter generated content in a way that evades detection by algorithms designed to identify and remove disinformation. This suggests that China is actively developing AI tools to circumvent efforts to combat online manipulation, potentially rendering existing safeguards ineffective.

Political Calculations and Silenced Warnings

Despite the gravity of these findings, Project Nightingale was reportedly shelved in early November after a series of high-level meetings between DG CONNECT officials and representatives from the European External Action Service (EEAS), the EU's diplomatic arm. Sources within the Commission claim that the EEAS expressed concerns that publishing the report would jeopardize ongoing trade negotiations with China and damage the EU's strategic relationship with Beijing.

One former DG CONNECT official, speaking on condition of anonymity, told *The Daily Signal* that Dr. Sharma and her team were deeply frustrated by the decision to suppress the report. "They had dedicated months to uncovering this threat, and they believed it was crucial for the EU to take immediate action to counter it. To have their work dismissed for purely political reasons was demoralizing," the official stated. The source further claimed that Dr. Sharma had repeatedly warned senior officials about the potential consequences of inaction, arguing that China's AI-driven disinformation campaigns posed a significant threat to European democracy. "She essentially argued that we were sacrificing our security for short-term economic gains."

"We cannot allow concerns about trade to blind us to the very real threat posed by AI-driven disinformation. The integrity of our democratic processes is at stake." — Source close to Dr. Anya Sharma

The Stakes for European Democracy

The suppression of Project Nightingale raises serious questions about the EU's commitment to protecting its democratic institutions from foreign interference. While concerns about maintaining a stable relationship with China are understandable, critics argue that prioritizing economic interests over security risks setting a dangerous precedent. By failing to publicly acknowledge and address the threat posed by China's AI-driven disinformation campaigns, the EU risks emboldening Beijing and undermining its own efforts to combat online manipulation.

Several members of the European Parliament have already voiced their concerns about the leaked report. Members of the INGE committee (Special Committee on foreign interference in all democratic processes in the European Union, including disinformation) are demanding a full investigation into the Commission's handling of Project Nightingale. They argue that the European public has a right to know the extent to which China is attempting to influence European politics and that the EU must take decisive action to protect its democratic institutions. The inquiry is expected to be a focal point in Brussels over the next few months.

The implications of Project Nightingale extend far beyond Europe. As AI technology continues to advance, the potential for AI-driven disinformation to destabilize democracies around the world will only increase. The EU's handling of this report will serve as a test case for how democracies respond to this emerging threat, and its actions will have far-reaching consequences for the future of global security.
